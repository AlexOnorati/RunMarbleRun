Setup

Note: If it's your first time playing, it is recommend to use cards 002-009 in step 2 and use events cards 1-3, 2-3, and 3-2.

1) Place the starter circuit card face down (card 001) in the middle of the play area face up. Also set aside card 010.

2) Shuffle all circuit cards (except card 001 and 010), Place them face down as a draw pile then draw 8 circuit cards face down and place them around the starter card. Forming a 3x3 grid with the starter card in the middle.

3) Rotate the cards in the grid randomly and flip over all cards int he grid.

4) When there are anyPower Cards place a directional pad or gate token on the card assoicated to the card.

5) Set the ability die, point tokens, and circuit cards to the side with room for a discard pile.

6) All players then pick a color and collect the matching spark, player markers and a victory point dial.

7) All players draw four circuit cards and add them to their hand. Cards in your hand are not revealed or shared with other players untill played.

8) Each player with their hand will tell the rest of the players the highest number they have on their card. They will be the first player and add circuit card 010 to their hand.

9) Randomly select a Level 1, Level 2 and Level 3 event card. Place them facedown in a stack with Level 1 on top, Level 2 middle and Level 3 on bottom. Do not reveal any of the events.

Circuit Cards

Card properties

1) Card number - Every card has unique number which is used for initial setup.

2) Wire Node - When entering a card you will place your spark to activate abilites and gain points.

3) Wire - The direction the spark will travel to get to the next card (colored line).

4) Point slot / Ability slot - Tells you what abiltiy to activate and where you place point tokens.

5) Directional Path - sparks can only travel one way by following the arrow to the exit of the card.

6) spark Ability - Abilites that can be claimed by players.

7) Starting Point - Place where you can put your spark to start the move phase.

Starting The Round

At the begining of the round flip over the topcard of the event deck. Read outloud the event and if needed, adjust the board according to the directions on the event card, additional rules on the event card overwrite base rules. Each player then collects two energy. The player with the first spark will take their turn first, (see During Your Turn section) after they have finished, players will take turns in a clockwise order.

During Your Turn

The turn has three phases that are played in order:

Move Phase:

1) Start your turn with two batteries.

2) Select a Starting Point noted by the spark shape and place your spark on the wire node next to the Starting Point.

3) The wire node is connected to ability slot and / or a point slot. If it's an ability slot, activate the Ability then place a point marker on the ability slot.

If it's a point slot, place a point marker on it.

You only will place one marker total on the wire unless you travel back to the same wire. You will then place a point token on an empty slot.

When all point slots are filled in and you visit the wire again, you do not place an aditional point token.

4) Repeat step 2 until your spark Overloads the circuit, reaches a dead end or runs off the edge of the current board. The move phase ends and you will move onto the collection phase.

When you pass a wire your spark has already crossed, you can place a point token on an open connected point slot.

Overloading

The move phase ends if your spark has entered four circuit cards with no point tokens added, no batteries spent, or marker tokens placed.

Dead End

The move phase ends if your spark enters a circuit card where the connected wire has a deadend. A deadend is marked by a wire path with cut corners.

Edge of the Board

The move phase ends if your spark exits the open end of a circuit card.

Collection Phase:

1) When there is any active flags each player collects a Victory Point for each active flag matching their player color. The color marker is not removed from the card. This step occurs at the end of each turn.

2) When there are any toll booths with point tokens on them, the player who owns that toll booth removes the point token and collects a Victory Point.

3) Collect all other point tokens on the board and add those points to your Victory Point tracker.

4) Remove your spark and all battery tokens on the board.

Object of Game

The goal of the game is to finish the game with the most Victory points. You can earn points by:

1) Placing point cubes on cards

2) Scoring extra points via the

When the last player has finished their turn, the round ends. A new event card is drawn for the new round (the old event is no longer in effect). Each player resets their energy tokens to two. The player with the first spark passes it to their left. The player with the first spark then starts the next round.

Note: In a two player game, the first spark does not get passed.

End of Round

When the last player has finished their turn, the round ends. A new event card is drawn for the new round (the old event is no longer in effect). Each player resets their energy tokens to two. The player with the first spark passes it to their left. The player with the first spark then starts the next round.

Note: In a two player game, the first spark does not get passed.

End of Game

The game ends when the fourth round is completed with the final event. All players count up their victory points, the player who has the most victory points wins! If there is a tie, whoever has the most active booths of the tie players win.

If there is still a tie, count active flags between the tie players whoever has more wins!

Power Cards

Power cards are noted having the symbol. The wire connected will also have a point slot to set points on. Power cards have different routes on how sparks pass through them. These routes can be changed by paying an energy token to alter the route. You can only use one energy on a power card per move phase, choose wisely!

Toggle Gate

A spark will move to the open wire path the gate is not blocking. Once the spark has past the gate, the gate's position is toggled to block the alternate wire.

An energy token can be spent to toggle the gate before or after the spark has passed the gate. The gate still toggles after the spark has passed.

Rotation Gate

A spark will move and follow the direction of the arrow on the roation gate.

Once the spark has past the gate, the gate will rotate 90 degrees clockwise.

An energy token can be spent to rotate the gate 90 degrees clockwise or counter clockwise. The gate still rotates 90 degress clockwise after the spark has passed it.

Triangle Gate

A spark will move to the open wire the gate is not blocking.

The spark will go straight if the wire is fully blocked by the gate. (Indicated by the arrow)

Once the spark has past the gate, flip the token so the diamond on the token and on the circuit card line up to create an alternate wire

An energy token can be spent to flip the gate before or after the spark has passed the gate. The gate still toggles after the spark has passed.

Directional Pad

A spark will move straight through a directional pad.


An energy token can be spent to change the direction the spark is moving including backwards!

Springs

Some power cards will have the symbol. When a spark passes this icon, it jumps over the circuit card to the next card. When a spring is paired with a directional pad or rotation gate you still follow the rules of that power card.

Wire Abilities

Almost all wiress have an ability associated to them. An ability is activated when a spark enters a Wire on the card.

Draw a Card


Draw a card from the draw pile and add it to your hand.

Place a Card


Place a card from your hand onto the maze, following build phase rules.

Rotate a Card


Pick the orientation the card in the maze. You cannot rotate the card with a spark on it.

Swap a Card


Draw a card into your hand and discard one into the discard pile.

Gain a Victory Point


Gain a Victory Point. You still place a point on the point slot.

Gain an Energy Token


Collect an energy token. This can only be used this turn.

Roll for Ability


Roll the die, activate the ability showing on the top side of the die.

Place a Flag


When passing a flag, place one of your player markers on top of the flag. If there is a different player marker already on the flag replace it. Point tokens are not placed on flags and player markers are not removed unless being replaced.

Place a Toll


Take one of your player markers and place it over the toll. When a spark passes the wire with the toll, place a point token on top of the toll booth matching the color with the owner. The player marker is not removed and stays till the end of the game.
