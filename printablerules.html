Setup

Place the starter circuit card (Card 001) in the middle of the play area with side A up. The board will be build out from this card so make plenty sure there is plenty of space.              Shuffle circuit cards 002-050, Place them face down as a draw pile then draw 8 circuit cards face down and place them around the starter card. Forming a 3x3 grid with the starter card in the middle. Rotate the cards in the grid randomly and flip over all cards in the grid. When there are any Power Cards place a power token on the card assoicated to the card. Set point tokens, and circuit cards (Draw pile)to the side. Draw cards until there are cards equal players plus one in the supply area. All players then pick a color and collect the matching spark, player markers and a victory point dial. Randomly select a Level 1, Level 2 and Level 3 event card. Place them facedown in a stack with Level 1 on top, Level 2 middle and Level 3 on bottom. Do not reveal any of the events.

The Game Round

The game is played in three rounds in each round the player takes a turn running their spark on the circuitboard earning points and activating abilities.

The game and first round starts with the player who has been shocked to most recent, if unknown allow the youngest player to go first.

The first player will flip over the top most event card in the stack and read it outloud. The event card overrides any base rule in the game. The first player will take their turn followed by the player to their left until all players have gone.

On Your Turn

On a turn, a player will collect two Battery tokens () then select a starting point ( ) Placing their spark on the wire node ( ) connected to the starting point.


Your spark will then follow the path wire to the final wire node on each wire card.


When the spark is placed on the wire node fill in the point slot connected to the wire with a point cube (). Ability slots are always filled in first and activated. Only one slot is filled per card. You can fill the other slot if you spark travels back to the wire later during the turn.

3

Wire Abilities

Almost all wires have an ability associated to them. An ability is activated when a spark is place on the wire that is connected to the wire.

Build a Card

Pick a card from the supply and add it to the board. A card has to be connected to to another card's edge so that the gray wires are connected. You can pick the rotation of the card on placement. If there are no cards in the supply, take the top card of the draw pile.

Rotate a Card

Pick the orientation of a card on the board. You cannot rotate the card that has your spark on it.

Gain a Victory Point

Add a point to your VP counter. You still place a point cube on the point slot.

Gain an Energy Token

Collect an energy token. This can only be used this turn.

Claim a Memory Slot

When passing a memory, place one of your player markers on top of the memory Slot. If there is a different player marker already on the Memory Slot replace it. Point tokens are not placed on the Memory Slot and player markers are not removed unless being replaced.

Claim a Capictor

The first time a Capictor is passed, take one of your player markers and place it over the Capictor Slot. Any future sparks pass the wire node with that Capictor, will place a point token on top of the capictor and player marker. The player marker is not removed and stays till the end of the game.

4

Power Cards

Power cards have battery slots () The wire connected will also have two point slots instead of a ability slot and a point slot.

Toggle Gate

When a toggle card is built, place a toggle gate () on the solid dots on the card. A spark will move to the open wire path the gate is not blocking.

Once the spark has past the gate, the gate's position is toggled to block the alternate wire. A battery () can be placed on the battery slot to toggle the gate. The gate will still toggle after the spark has passed.

Rotation Gate

When a toggle card is built, place a rotation gate      () on the on the card aligning the four dots making sure the solid dots are aligned. A spark will move and follow the direction of the arrow on the rotation gate.

Once the spark has past the gate, the gate will rotate 90 degrees clockwise. A battery () can be placed on the battery slot to rotate the gate 90 degrees clockwise or counter clockwise. The gate still rotates 90 degress clockwise after the spark has passed.


5

Ending your Turn

Your spark will continue to move through the circuit as long as your spark is charged. A spark looses it's charge if your spark encounters a dead end, exits an open card, or overloads by passing circuit cards already visited.

Dead End

A dead end is noted by the cut corners. A spark will loose it's charge when it enters the deadend.

Edge of the Board

The spark looses it's charge when it exits a wire path with no connected wire path connected.

Overloading

A spark looses it's charge when it enters four circuit cards consecutively with no point tokens, batteries, or player markers placed.

6

Collecting Points

After a spark has lost it's charge, it's time to collect points and reset the board for the next player's turn.

Memory Slot

Each player collects a Victory Point for each active memory slot they have a player token on.

Capictor Slot

Each player collects a Victory Point for each capictor slot they have a player token and a point token on. Remove the point token but leave the player token.

Collect Point Tokens

Collect all other point tokens on the board and add those points to current player's Victory Point tracker.

Circuit Board Reset

The current player removes their spark and all battery tokens are discarded on the board and with the current player. Cards are drawn into the supply until the supply has as many cards as players plus one extra. If the draw pile is empty, nothing happens.

End of Round

When the last player has finished their turn, the round ends. The current event card is discarded and the next one in the stack is flipped over.

The first player will then begin the next round.

End of Game

The game ends when the third round is complete. The player who has the most victory points wins! If there is a tie, whoever has the most active capictors of the tie players wins! If there is still a tie, count active memory slots between the tie players whoever has more wins!

Alternate Play

Once you have played a few times, we recommend doing some alternate play! You can mix and match all alternate modes.

Triangle Gate

Add cards 051-070

When a Triangle Gate card is built, place a Triangle gate. ()

A spark will move to the open wire the gate is not blocking. The spark will go straight if the wire is fully blocked by the gate. (Indicated by the arrow)

Once the spark has past the gate, the gate's position is toggled by flipping the gate. A battery () can be placed on the battery slot to toggle the gate. The gate will still toggle after the spark has passed.

Directional Pad

Add cards 071-080

When a Directional card is built, place a Directional Pad () on the card. A spark will move straight through the card. A battery () can be placed on the battery slot to pick the direction of the spark will go, including the way the spark came from!

Springs

Add cards 081-100 also add event card R3:E3 for setup.

Some rotational gate and directional pad cards will now have the symbol. When a spark passes this icon, it will skip the adjacent circuit card and continue onto the next card in that direction. This can be used to skip a max a gab of one. When a spark travels over a spring and does not land back on the board it has considered to loose it's charge.
